Battery and its Types ( English )

Battery Types

There are two types of batteries, lithium-ion and neon-ion. Lithium-ion is made with new technology, it uses paper technology, which has a negative electrode on one side and a positive electrode on the other side, while the old technology battery, i.e. Neo-Nine, uses a carbon rod and silicon to form a cell. Both of these types have charging cells and non-charging cells. The mobile battery has three point seven DC volts and its power is counted according to milliampere, the more milliampere in the battery, the more its power will be and the battery time of the mobile will be longer. Its milliamperes are selected by the hardware engineers of the mobile manufacturer. There are three types of points on the battery

  1. - / +
  2. - / BSI / +
  3. - / Temp / BSI / +

The function of BSI i.e. battery status indicator is when the charging of the battery is complete, the mobile gets a message that the charging is complete, in this case the charging symbol stops on the display. The function of the temperature point, When the mobile is charging, due to the flow of current, the battery starts getting heat, then this point helps to keep the battery cool. If the timing of the battery starts to vary, it means that the battery The cells begin to turn into carbon which slowly hardens the battery. This is confirmed by pressing the battery. If the battery life is short, its charging time will also be short. If the charger is damaged or the battery is overcharged, there is a risk of the battery swelling.

Battery Boosting

First, check the DC volts of the battery with a multimeter. If the DC voltage reading is less than three point seven volts, shock it or leave it charging for a while. A DC power supply is required to shock the battery. Adjust the DC power supply to shock and place the positive wire on the positive point of the battery and the negative wire on the negative point of the battery and hold for seven to eight seconds. Keep it on.

Tips and Facts
  • The life of the mobile phone is maintained by keeping it on. If a mobile is to be kept off for a long time, leave it on airplane mode and after ten or fifteen days when the battery is dead, charge it and put it back.
  • If the BISI point is damaged, the battery will not work.
  • Overheating the battery can cause the battery to explode.
  • The positive point of the battery is always next to the BSI point and in ninety-five percent of mobile phones the positive point of the battery terminal is next to its boundary line.
  • Every charger adapter has a converter circuit inside it that converts AC to DC and gives it to the mobile.
  • Most mobile phones or their batteries have a white water damage indicator sticker. When this sticker turns red, it means that the battery has been damaged by water or there is a problem with the battery or the mobile phone. .
