Sections of Mobile Board ( English )

Every mobile always has three sections.

  1. CPU Section
  2. Signal Section
  3. Transmitting Section

The lines on the mobile board are gold in color. These lines are called earth lines, negative lines or ground lines. These lines provide negative voltage to the parts of the mobile board. If the circuit of any part is broken, a thin jumper wire is used to replace it. is attached which is a thin copper wire.

1. CPU Section

This part is called the brain or control room of the mobile and all the functions of the mobile are controlled from here. Its distinguishing feature is that this section has more number of black colored ICs.

  • Charging Resister

    • A charging resistor is a component that checks the voltage coming from the charging IC if it is as per the battery requirement and then lets it go if this component is damaged then charging will not take place. Codes are written

    R220,R224,R221,R222,R39,R58

    And this IC will always be placed next to or behind the charging IC and its circuit will be connected to the positive point of the battery.

  • Terminals and Interfaces

    • All the points or pins which we can change or move all the pins are called terminal pins and the places where the terminals are connected which we cannot change are called interfaces.

  • RTC (Real Time Clock)

    • It is a component that keeps track of the date and time of the mobile. It operates the date and time of the mobile.

  • CPU IC

    • It is an IC that supplies all the components in the mobile according to their requirements. If it gets damaged, the mobile is dead. But this IC is replaceable. C-cont would be written. After the CPU, the second major chip is the IC.

  • Audio IC

    • This audio IC controls two things in the mobile, one is the speaker and the other is the microphone. Its distinguishing feature is that it is connected to the IC direct or indirect mic and speaker interface.

  • Flash IC

    • This is called the hard disk of the mobile, it contains the records of the mobile, it is known that it is longer than all ICs. In new mobiles, this IC is shiny or silver in color. Even if there are two, they will be of the same size.

  • RAM IC

    • This is an IC that is called the CPUIC's assistant IC. Its distinguishing feature is that it is always the second longest IC after the Flash IC. If the Flash IC is bright, the RAM IC will Can be long or short and it can be bright if it is bright it will be smaller than flash IC.

  • DSP (Digital Signal Processing)

    • This IC controls the radio in the mobile and connects the SIM to its network. If this IC is damaged, it will come up with NO NETWORK written. It is attached to it.

  • EEPROM

    • Mobiles that don't have a DSPIC have this IC that connects the SIM to the network, some mobiles have both of these ICs.

2. Signal Section

The signal section is a section that controls incoming and outgoing signals in the mobile and also makes them powerful, i.e. fulfills their megahertz and also controls different bands of signals. There are three types of signal bands. Single Band Dual Band and Tri Band

  • Single band = 900MHz , 1800MHz , 850MHz
  • Double Band = 900/1800MHz , 900/850MHz , 1800/850MHz
  • Tri band = 900/1800/850MHz

  • 900MHz = Zong , Telenor , Ufone
  • 1800MHz = Jazz , Warid
  • 850MHz = 4G Network supports

Its distinguishing feature is that the section which has at least one and at most two ICs and has a large number of white and silver color filters is called the signal section. It has two types of ICs. R-FIC and Mixer IC.

  • RF IC

    • It controls the entire system of signals in the mobile and the RFIC is called the manager IC of the signal section, it is always placed in the middle of the signal section.

  • Mixer IC

    • It is the assistant IC of the RFIC and is placed on the right or left side of the signal section.

  • Filters

    • Their job is to clean the incoming and outgoing signals. There are two types of them, one is the RX filter and the other is the TX filter.

    Rx Filters

    It is the filter which is placed directly on the RFIC, it cleans the incoming signals and is called receiving signal filter.

    Tx Filters

    These filters are placed on the opposite side of the RFIC, they clean the outgoing signals and are called transmitting signal filters.

  • Types of Filters

    • There are three types of filters depending on the size, one is VCO filter and the other is crystal oscillator and the third type is normal filter.

    VCO Filters

    These are filters that are larger than all other filters.

    Crystal Oscillator Filters

    These filters are smaller than VCOs, but not too small.

    Normal Filters

    These are the filters that are the smallest in size.

3. Transmitting Section

This is a section that controls the incoming and outgoing signals and includes the following components,

  1. Antinna
  2. External Antinna
  3. PA (Power Amplifier) IC
  4. DA (Driver Amplifier) IC
  5. Duplexer or Antinna Switch

  • Antinna

    • The function of the antenna is to send the signals inside the mobile or outside the mobile.

  • External Antinna

    • The external antenna is silver or golden in color and is placed very clearly on the back side of the mobile. If the signal is not coming in the room, connect it with a jumper wire from the antenna of the TV, then the signal will start coming.

  • PA (Power Amplifier) IC

    • This is an IC that sends the outgoing signals by making it powerful. It will be the biggest IC of this section. It has been seen in three forms so far, one is a leg IC, and the second is a black bubble IC and the third is a silver core bubble.
